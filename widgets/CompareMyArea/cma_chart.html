The National Emissions Inventory (NEI) is EPAâ€™s comprehensive emissions inventory. It is used to support air quality modeling and other activities within the Air Toxics Program. The 2014 NEI, after review by State, Local, and Tribal agencies, was used as input to the AERMOD and CMAQ models to generate ambient concentrations.








The ambient concentrations were used as input to the inhalation exposure model (HAPEM7) to generate exposure concentrations. Exposure modeling is an important step in this assessment because it takes into account that people move from one location to another (e.g., from outside environments to inside environments).








Exposure concentrations were used with health-benchmark information to estimate risks or hazards.
