In the Gridded Map Tool, select a unit area from the dropdown.

  • Draw an area – create a freehand drawing in the map.
  • Congressional District – select a Congressional District.
  • HUC-12 – select a HUC12 subwatershed. Note: zoom in to view HUC boundaries.
  • County – select a county.
  • Specific Point – create a buffer area around a selected point.

Select the national indicator to summarize. At this time, the 2012 National Land Cover Dataset (NLCD) is the only national indicator layer available.

Create or Select the unit area interactively in the map.

  • Create an area of interest on the map using the Draw an area or Specific Point options:
    • To Draw an area, click the mouse button and hold down to draw the area of interest in the map. Let go of the mouse button to finish the drawing.
    • To use a Specific Point, enter a Buffer distance and units in the tool. Click on the desired point on the map. The analysis area is a circle, centered on the specific point, with a radius equal to the buffer distance.
  • Select an area of interest on the map using the Congressional District, HUC-12, or County options:
    • Click the selected unit area from the layer in the map.

The EnviroAtlas map shows the selected unit area with the national indicator clipped to it. To calculate national indicator statistics within the unit area, click Calculate.

Review the statistics in the Gridded Map Tool's Results tab. Click View Full Legend to open the NLCD legend in a separate browser tab.
